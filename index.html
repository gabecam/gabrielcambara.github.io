Site Visit Reflections

What the room
actually teaches you.

Morgan Stanley

Visiting Morgan Stanley offered an unusually direct window into what it actually means to work in institutional finance — beyond the textbook version. What struck me most was the emphasis on client relationship management as a genuine skill in its own right, separate from and equal to technical ability. The professionals were clear that models and pitch materials are table stakes; what differentiates bankers over a career is judgment, communication under pressure, and the ability to build lasting trust with clients.

The visit reinforced the value of specificity in my own recruiting. Candidates who demonstrate genuine knowledge of a group's deal flow — not just the firm's brand — stand out in a way generic applications cannot replicate. This directly shaped how I structure my Moelis outreach, leading with specific transaction knowledge rather than broad interest statements.

I came away with greater conviction about pursuing banking. The professionals were honest about the tradeoffs and it still felt like the right fit.

SVEM — Silicon Valley Elite Manufacturing

The SVEM visit was a meaningful counterpoint to Morgan Stanley. Where the Morgan Stanley conversation centered on established institutional processes, SVEM illustrated how capital deployment works at the frontier — in markets where information is thin, relationships carry outsized weight, and the conventional deal-sourcing playbook has to be rebuilt from scratch.

What I found most interesting was how SVEM identifies opportunities larger funds overlook due to scale constraints or geographic unfamiliarity — a dynamic I recognized from my True Purpose Capital work, searching for defensible niches in fragmented industries.

This visit sharpened my appreciation for thesis-driven investing. The SVEM team was not reacting to deal flow; they were generating it by holding strong sector convictions and building relationships ahead of the capital need — a posture I am applying in my own recruiting, cultivating banker relationships 18 months before I need an offer.
